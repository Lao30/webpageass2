Street Kids School Project

A community driven education program that provides free, informal schooling for street children in Bali. With the help of volunteers and donors, we offer safe learning spaces, life skills training, and government equivalency education to help children break the cycle of poverty and build a brighter future.

Regular Program

Kejar Paket Class

Kejar Paket Class is a dedicated learning program designed to help street children in Bali catch up on formal education through Indonesia’s government equivalency pathways—Paket A (elementary), Paket B (middle school), and Paket C (high school). These classes provide a second chance for children who cannot attend regular schools due to unstable life circumstances.

Classes are held every Monday from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM, offering a consistent, supportive, and child-friendly environment where students can learn at their own pace. This program is made possible through a meaningful collaboration with partnering organizations who share our vision of providing access to quality education for every child, no matter their background.

Class from Volunteer

Volunteer Class is an open and flexible learning program where local and international volunteers share their time, knowledge, and creativity with the children. Each volunteer brings their own unique lesson to the class—ranging from basic reading, writing, and math to English practice, singing, drawing, simple science experiments, crafts, and more.
This class allows children to explore various fun and educational activities in a relaxed and joyful environment, while also helping them build confidence and curiosity. We welcome volunteers from all backgrounds who are passionate about teaching and making a difference in the lives of Bali’s street children.
